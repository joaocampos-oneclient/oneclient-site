## The Myth of the General-Purpose AI

Every founder we talk to wants the same thing: an AI that understands their business. The problem is that "understanding" is not a feature you can bolt on. It requires deep vertical knowledge, custom data pipelines, and months of iteration.

We learned this the hard way. Our first attempt was a horizontal platform — a general-purpose AI layer that any business could plug into. It worked in demos. It failed in production.

## What Actually Works

The breakthrough came when we stopped trying to be general and started going deep. Each vertical we entered taught us something new about what production AI actually requires:

- **Data quality matters more than model size.** A well-curated dataset with 10K examples outperforms a noisy dataset with 1M examples every time.
- **Latency is a feature.** When your AI takes 8 seconds to respond, users leave. When it responds in 800ms, they forget they're talking to a machine.
- **The last mile is the hardest.** Getting an AI to 80% accuracy is easy. Getting it to 95% requires domain expertise that no amount of prompt engineering can replace.

## The Infrastructure Bet

We're betting that the future belongs to companies that own their vertical AI infrastructure — not companies that rent generic models from cloud providers. The economics are clear: custom infrastructure costs more upfront but compounds over time. Generic APIs get cheaper but never get smarter about your specific domain.

This is why we build with partners who know their markets. They bring the domain knowledge. We bring the engineering. Together, we build something that neither could build alone.
